Labrador Retriever

The Labrador Retriever is the most popular dog breed in the United States.

  • Personality: Friendly and very energetic
  • Average Adult Height: 22-24 inches (male), 21-23 inches (female)
  • Average Adult Weight: 65-80 lbs (male), 55-70 lbs (female)
  • Life Expectancy: 10-12 years
  • Country of Origin: Canada

According to the American Kennel Club, the Lab has held the designation as America's favorite dog breed now for 26 consecutive years. The Labrador Retriever that we know of today is actually is a descendent of the "St. John's Water Dog", which is a cross-breed of the small water dogs that populated Newfoundland, Canada and the Newfoundland dog breed. Labs are strong swimmers and great hunting companions. Because of their intelligence and trainability, Labs often serve as guide dogs, therapy dogs, or service dogs. The Lab was officially recognized by the American Kennel Club in 1917.
